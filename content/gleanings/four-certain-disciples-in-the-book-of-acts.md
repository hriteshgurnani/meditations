---
title: Four “Certain” Disciples in the Book of Acts
---

The book of Acts does not only highlight apostles and public leaders. It also draws our attention to four **“certain disciples”** — whose quiet faithfulness was precious to God and useful in His purposes.

---

## 1. Ananias — A Disciple Ready to Speak

> “And there was a certain disciple at Damascus, named Ananias; and to him said the Lord in a vision, Ananias. And he said, Behold, I am here, Lord.”  
> — Acts 9:10

At a time when Saul was making havoc of the church, God chose an otherwise unknown disciple. Ananias was neither prominent nor public, yet he was available, obedient, and courageous.

His first words to Saul — **“Brother Saul”** (v.17) — reveal a heart shaped by grace rather than fear.

God used him to speak life and assurance to a man others dreaded. Ananias was used by the Lord to speak to another.

---

## 2. Tabitha (Dorcas) — A Disciple Full of Good Works

> “Now there was at Joppa a certain disciple named Tabitha, which by interpretation is called Dorcas: this woman was full of good works and almsdeeds which she did.”  
> — Acts 9:36

Tabitha’s faith was expressed through consistent acts of love.

She was **“full of good works and almsdeeds,”** and her death brought an outpouring of grief — she was deeply missed.

Peter was shown a display of one woman’s labour, which was done for the glory of God and the good of others. The narrative points out her deliverance in verse 40, being raised from the dead and allowing her to continue serving.

She was used by the Lord to help others practically.

---

## 3. Timothy — A Young Disciple Entrusted with Responsibility

> “Then came he to Derbe and Lystra: and, behold, a certain disciple was there, named Timotheus, the son of a certain woman, which was a Jewess, and believed; but his father was a Greek.”  
> — Acts 16:1

Timothy was young, yet spiritually grounded through a godly heritage.

> “When I call to remembrance the unfeigned faith that is in thee, which dwelt first in thy grandmother Lois, and thy mother Eunice...”  
> — 2 Timothy 1:5

He knew the Scriptures from childhood:

> “And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures...”  
> — 2 Timothy 3:15

Timothy became Paul’s trusted companion and spiritual son. Paul saw in him a vessel prepared to carry the work forward, entrusting him with responsibility and encouragement.

---

## 4. Mnason — An Old Disciple Still Serving

> “There went with us also certain of the disciples of Caesarea, and brought with them one Mnason of Cyprus, an old disciple, with whom we should lodge.”  
> — Acts 21:16

Mnason appears only once in Scripture, yet his simple act of hospitality is eternally recorded.

Described as an **“old disciple,”** he reminds us that there is no retirement from the Lord’s work.

Even quiet, unseen service — offering a home, encouragement, or prayer — is precious to God.

---

These certain disciples were different in age, gift, and sphere, yet all were available to the Lord.

- **Ananias** teaches us to speak for Christ when called.
- **Tabitha** shows us how love for Christ expresses itself in practical care.
- **Timothy** encourages the young to be teachable and willing.
- **Mnason** reminds us to remain faithful and fruitful to the end.

May we feel the challenge of these **“certain disciples”** — whether a brother or sister, young or old — and may we serve the Lord faithfully, for His glory.
