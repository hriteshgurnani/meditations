---
title: Four Little Things, Four Great Lessons
---

How often we imagine that it is only the big things which count; when frequently it is the very opposite.

Below are four little things in the Bible that teach us four great spiritual lessons.

---

## 1. A Little Sleep

> “Yet a little sleep, a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep: So shall thy poverty come as one that travelleth, and thy want as an armed man.”  
> — Proverbs 6:10–11

Solomon draws the picture of a man who wants to sleep a little longer than he should; and it turns out to be a deep, heavy sleep. He becomes so fond of it that he repeats it again and again.

The result is that he is reduced to poverty.

It was pleasant at first, but in reality it was a disaster.

Isn't that often the way with sin? It appears pleasant, but in the end it brings destruction.

We remember the story of Jonah. He tried to run away from God, and we read:

> “But Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship; and he lay, and was fast asleep.”  
> — Jonah 1:5

He was completely unaware of the storm around him and had to be awakened.

Sin is a terrible thing.

> “Awake thou that sleepest, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall give thee light.”  
> — Ephesians 5:14

---

## 2. A Little Stone

> “And he took his staff in his hand, and chose him five smooth stones out of the brook...”  
> — 1 Samuel 17:40

David, the young shepherd, selected five little stones from the brook. Little did he know that behind one of those stones was the destiny of a nation.

It became the stone of victory.

One blow and the battle was over. The champion of the Philistines was defeated, and the fearful army of Israel became a conquering band.

All through a little stone — plus God.

The victory did not lie in the size of the stone but in the greatness of David’s God.

We also read of:

> “A great stone”  
> — Matthew 27:60

But that great stone was rolled away, declaring that the greatest victory had been accomplished.

As David defeated Goliath, so the Lord Jesus Christ, through His death and resurrection, defeated Satan and overthrew his kingdom.

The empty grave declares that His victory was perfect.

Sin has been punished in our Substitute, and our sins, like a great army, have been swallowed up in His victory.

---

## 3. A Little Servant

> “And the Syrians had gone out by companies, and had brought away captive out of the land of Israel a little maid; and she waited on Naaman’s wife. And she said unto her mistress, Would God my lord were with the prophet that is in Samaria! for he would recover him of his leprosy.”  
> — 2 Kings 5:2–3

We do not know her name, but her life shines out and will never be forgotten.

She could have sat down and cried until her heart was broken. She could have tried to escape her circumstances.

But she did something far better.

### She was brave

She refused to be discouraged by her circumstances.

### She was bold

She refused to remain silent, even though she was far away from home.

Quietly, she waited for an opportunity to speak for her Lord. Her master was suffering, yet her faith rose above every difficulty.

### She was blessed

One day she saw Naaman returning home as a changed man, completely healed — and all through her little word of testimony.

Indeed, we are also called:

> “Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.”  
> — Matthew 5:16

---

## 4. A Little Spider

> “The spider taketh hold with her hands, and is in kings’ palaces.”  
> — Proverbs 30:28

The spider is one of the four little things which are **“exceeding wise.”**

It teaches us valuable lessons through its diligence and perseverance.

How industrious this little creature is. How patiently it works, and how carefully its work is accomplished.

Notice what Solomon specially mentions:

> “The spider taketh hold with her hands.”

She does not sit idle. She gets to work. Quickly she fastens her silken threads and patiently builds her web.

She takes hold and continues until her work is complete.

Have we truly taken hold of Christ in our lives?

Let us:

> “Fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on eternal life...”  
> — 1 Timothy 6:12

---

God delights to teach great truths through little things.

- A **little sleep** warns us against spiritual neglect.
- A **little stone** reminds us that God delights to use weak things to accomplish great victories.
- A **little servant** shows the power of a faithful testimony.
- A **little spider** teaches perseverance and steadfast faith.

May we never despise the little things, for God often accomplishes His greatest work through them.
