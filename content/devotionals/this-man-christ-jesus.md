# A sevenfold testimony to the Lord Jesus Christ from the lips of men—and from God Himself.

Throughout the New Testament, the Lord Jesus is repeatedly referred to simply as **"this Man."** Spoken by many different witnesses, these words unfold the glory of His Person and the greatness of His work.

---

## 1. "Never man spake like this man." - The Officers  

> **The officers answered, Never man spake like this man.**<br>
> -- John 7:46

---

## 2. "This man receiveth sinners." - The Pharisees and Scribes  

> **And the Pharisees and scribes murmured, saying, This man receiveth sinners, and eateth with them.**<br>
> -- Luke 15:2

---

## 3. "This man hath done nothing amiss." - The Dying Robber

> **And we indeed justly; for we receive the due reward of our deeds: but this man hath done nothing amiss.**<br>
> -- Luke 23:41

---

## 4. "Truly this man was the Son of God." - The Roman Centurion  

> **And when the centurion, which stood over against him, saw that he so cried out, and gave up the ghost, he said, Truly this man was the Son of God.**<br>
> -- Mark 15:39

---

## 5. "Not this man, but Barabbas." - The People

> **Then cried they all again, saying, Not this man, but Barabbas. Now Barabbas was a robber.**<br>
> -- John 18:40

---

## 6. "This man was counted worthy..." - The Holy Spirit concerning the Father's estimate of the Son  

> **For this man was counted worthy of more glory than Moses, inasmuch as he who hath builded the house hath more honour than the house.**<br>
> -- Hebrews 3:3

---

## 7. "Through this man is preached unto you the forgiveness of sins." - Paul

> **Be it known unto you therefore, men and brethren, that through this man is preached unto you the forgiveness of sins:**<br>
> -- Acts 13:38

Every witness saw **"this Man,"** yet each saw Him differently.<br>
Some admired Him, some rejected Him, some confessed Him, and some proclaimed Him. 

Together they present a beautiful sevenfold testimony to the incomparable Lord Jesus Christ.
