---
title: The Cross From God’s Viewpoint
---

The death of Christ was an act between the Father and the Son, carried out in the eternal counsel of God. While it was for man’s good, we should never forget that it was also for God’s glory. The great aim of God in Christ was not only reconciling the world to Himself but also the glorifying of His name.

Through Christ and His finished work on the Cross:

## 1. God Magnified His Law Through Christ’s Obedience and Death

The Lord Jesus kept the law of God in a world of sin. He never deviated from it but was obedient to the very last letter and thereby honoured God’s law where Adam had dishonoured it.

He was even willing to hang on the Cross in agony a little longer, **“that the scripture might be fulfilled.”**

---

## 2. God Magnified His Love by Sending His Only Begotten Son for Us

He did not withdraw the Saviour from this world, but gave Him up to death, to demonstrate His love — love which is unchangeable, incomprehensible and unfathomable.

> “Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us, and sent His Son to be the propitiation for our sins.”  
> — 1 John 4:10

---

## 3. God Magnified His Justice Through Christ

When the Lord Jesus made Himself sin for us, God called upon the sword to smite Him.

> “Awake, O sword, against my shepherd, and against the man that is my fellow, saith the LORD of hosts...”  
> — Zechariah 13:7

Justice and judgement are the foundation of God’s throne.

> “Justice and judgment are the habitation of thy throne: mercy and truth shall go before thy face.”  
> — Psalm 89:14

Mercy can be righteously extended to sinners only because God’s righteous demands were fully met at the Cross.

---

## 4. God Magnified His Holiness Through Christ’s Death

The death of the Lord Jesus on the Cross shows God’s holy hatred for sin.

The Cross reveals how awful sin must be and the price that was paid for it.

---

## 5. God Magnified His Truth Through Christ

All His promises and prophecies are fulfilled concerning Christ and the Cross.

He will yet magnify His truth by fulfilling the whole of His promises to His Son, who in the present time is waiting for His enemies to be made His footstool.

> “Sit thou at my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool.”  
> — Psalm 110:1

---

## 6. God Magnified His Wisdom Through Christ

God showed His ability to deal with the question of sin in justice and yet in love and mercy.

His plan of redemption reveals a wisdom that deserves awe. No created mind could have conceived such a plan.

> “O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past finding out!”  
> — Romans 11:33

---

## 7. God Magnified His Power Through Christ

> “For though he was crucified through weakness, yet he liveth by the power of God.”  
> — 2 Corinthians 13:4

How great was His sustaining power, especially when we consider that He was **“crucified in weakness.”**

If such power was displayed through voluntary weakness, how strong must He be!

---

## 8. God Magnified His Grace Through Christ

To reconcile man back to Himself, God allowed His Holy Son to have the iniquity of us all laid upon Him.

> “The LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all.”  
> — Isaiah 53:6

> “For ye know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that, though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor, that ye through his poverty might be rich.”  
> — 2 Corinthians 8:9

---

## 9. God Magnified His Mercy by Receiving Sinners on the Ground of the Finished Work of the Cross

Because of Christ’s finished work, salvation is freely offered to all who call upon Him.

> “For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.”

There is no difference between Jew and Greek, rich and poor, or any other distinction.

> “That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved.”  
> — Romans 10:9

---

In the Lord Jesus Christ and the finished work of the Cross, God has made known:

- The riches of His grace
- The triumph of His justice
- The perfection of His holiness
- The wisdom of His counsel
- The power of His salvation

> “For of him, and through him, and to him, are all things: to whom be glory for ever. Amen.”

> “Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing.”
