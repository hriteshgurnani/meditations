---
title: Three "We Haves" in Hebrews
---
One of the beautiful expressions repeated throughout the book of Hebrews is the phrase, **"we have."**<br> 
The believer's life is not built upon what he hopes to obtain someday, but upon what he already possesses in Christ.

The writer points us to three precious possessions that belong to every believer:

- We have a **Great High Priest**.
- We have a **Hope**.
- We have an **Altar**.

These three "we haves" provide everything the believer needs for his journey.<br> 
They give us **support**, **security**, and **service**.

---

## 1. We Have a Great High Priest - Our Support

> **Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our profession.**  
> -- Hebrews 4:14

The first "we have" directs our attention **upward**.<br>
We have not been left to walk the Christian pathway alone. We have a living High Priest who has passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God.<br>
Throughout the epistle, the greatness of the priesthood of the Lord Jesus is progressively unfolded.

He is:
- Merciful and Faithful.
- Great High Priest.
- Touched with the feeling of our infirmities.
- Holy.
- Harmless.
- Undefiled.
- Separate from sinners.
- Higher than the heavens.
- Seated at God's right hand.

Because He ever lives, His priesthood never changes.<br>
Therefore, the writer exhorts us,

> **Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.**<br>
> -- Hebrews 4:6

Our support is not found in our own strength, but in our Great High Priest.

---

## 2. We Have a Hope - Our Security

> **"Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within the veil;"**  
> -- Hebrews 6:19

The second "we have" directs our attention **forward**—to a Hope.

The believer is not left drifting through life with uncertain expectations. Our hope is anchored beyond the veil where Christ Himself has entered.

It is:
- Sure
- Steadfast
- Anchored in heaven
- Grounded upon God's unchangeable promise
- Secured by Christ our Forerunner

Under the Old Covenant, the high priest entered the Holiest as a representative.<br>
But the Lord Jesus entered as our **Forerunner**, guaranteeing that His people shall one day follow Him into the Father's presence.

Our security rests not upon ourselves, but upon Him who has already entered within the veil.

---

## 3. We Have an Altar - Our Service

> **We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle.**  
> -- Hebrews 13:10

The third "we have" directs our attention **Godward**.<br>
The believer possesses an altar unlike that of the Old Covenant.<br>
It is not material.<br>
It is not ceremonial.<br>
It is not connected with earthly ritual.<br>

It is a spiritual privilege enjoyed through Christ Himself.

Immediately after speaking of the altar, the writer says,

> **By Him therefore let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks to His name.**<br>
> Hebrews 13:15

Our altar leads to worship.<br>
Our privilege leads to praise.<br>
Our access leads to service.

---

The Epistle to the Hebrews reminds us of three precious possessions that belong to every child of God:

- **We have a Great High Priest** — our continual **Support**.
- **We have a Hope** — our abiding **Security**.
- **We have an Altar** — our joyful **Service**.

These three "we haves" direcst the believer's life:

- **Upward** — to our Great High Priest.
- **Forward** — to our sure Hope.
- **Godward** — to our Altar.

The Christian life is never lived alone, never lived uncertainly, and never lived without worship.

Because of what **we have** in Christ, we may come boldly, rest confidently, and serve continually.
